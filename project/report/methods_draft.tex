\section{Methodology}\label{sec:meth}

Our objective is to test where the independent-site Fermi--Dirac (FD)
framework of Wagih et al.~\cite{Wagih2020} for grain-boundary (GB) solute
segregation breaks down in $(T, X_c)$ space, where $T$ is the temperature
and $X_c$ the global Mg fraction. The methodology is structured as a
hypothesis-versus-ground-truth comparison on a single shared atomistic
basis (Fig.~\ref{fig:method}): the FD framework provides the prediction
$X_\mathrm{GB}^\mathrm{FD}(T, X_c)$, hybrid Monte Carlo / molecular
dynamics (HMC) sampling provides the ground truth
$X_\mathrm{GB}^\mathrm{HMC}(T, X_c)$, and the gap between them is the
testable quantity. Sub-sections below describe each leg in turn; key
equations and the HMC algorithm are stated in
Apps.~\ref{app:eq}--\ref{app:alg}.


\subsection{Atomistic base and segregation-energy spectrum}\label{sec:meth:base}

A 200~\AA{} cubic Al polycrystal of $N_\mathrm{tot} = 475{,}715$ atoms is
built by 3D Voronoi tessellation of 16 randomly placed grain seeds with
uniform SO(3) orientations, with each FCC lattice site assigned to its
nearest seed under periodic boundary conditions; the resulting box is
then annealed using the Wagih protocol~\cite{Wagih2020} in
LAMMPS~\cite{LAMMPS} with the Mendelev Al--Mg embedded-atom-method
potential~\cite{Mendelev2009} (full anneal schedule in
App.~\ref{app:atomistic}). Adaptive common-neighbour
analysis~\cite{Stukowski2012} identifies $N_\mathrm{GB} = 89{,}042$ GB
atoms, giving a GB fraction $f_\mathrm{GB} \equiv
N_\mathrm{GB}/N_\mathrm{tot} = 0.187$. The resulting GB mask is fixed
throughout, so that all downstream quantities — the $\Delta E_i$ spectrum,
the FD predictor, and the HMC measurement — refer to the same set of
sites.

The per-site segregation energy $\Delta E_i$ at GB site $i$
[Eq.~(\ref{eq:dE})] is the energy change for transferring a single Mg
from a bulk substitutional site to GB site $i$, computed at $T = 0$~K
with full local relaxation. We sample $n = 500$ random GB sites; this
sub-sample passes a two-sample Kolmogorov--Smirnov test against the
Wagih Zenodo dataset and is sufficient for the canonical FD predictor
(App.~\ref{app:dE_compare}).


\subsection{Fermi--Dirac equilibrium predictions}\label{sec:meth:FD}

We compute two FD prediction variants from the same $\{\Delta E_i\}$
spectrum:
\begin{enumerate}\itemsep0pt\parsep0pt
    \item \emph{Grand-canonical} (GC-FD)~[Eq.~(\ref{eq:FD_single})]: the
        textbook Wagih form with chemical-potential offset $\Delta\mu$
        fixed by the bulk reference. It does not enforce $N_\mathrm{Mg}$
        conservation.
    \item \emph{Canonical} (canon-FD)~[Eq.~(\ref{eq:FD_canon})]:
        $\Delta\mu$ self-consistent under the closed-box constraint
        $N_\mathrm{Mg} = X_c \cdot N_\mathrm{tot}$.
\end{enumerate}
Canon-FD is the appropriate comparator for our HMC measurement, which is
also closed-box at fixed $X_c$. GC-FD is reported alongside as the
upper envelope of the FD prediction band, and as the point of reference
for readers familiar with Wagih's textbook curve. The two coincide in
the dilute limit but diverge as the GB fills.


\subsection{Hybrid Monte Carlo / MD sampling}\label{sec:meth:HMC}

To compute $X_\mathrm{GB}$ at finite $X_c$ in the presence of
solute--solute interactions, we use the LAMMPS \texttt{fix atom/swap}
implementation~\cite{Sadigh2012} of canonical Metropolis swap Monte
Carlo interleaved with NVT molecular dynamics --- hereafter
\emph{HMC}\footnote{Hybrid Monte Carlo / molecular dynamics. Distinct
from the Hamiltonian Monte Carlo of Duane \emph{et al.},
\emph{Phys.~Lett.~B} \textbf{195}, 216 (1987); here HMC denotes the
MC--MD hybrid implemented by LAMMPS \texttt{fix atom/swap}.}. The
integration timestep is $\Delta t = 1$~fs. Every 100 timesteps
($0.1$~ps), 100 Mg$\leftrightarrow$Al type-swap moves are attempted,
each independently Metropolis-accepted at the simulation temperature
$T$; this preserves the closed-box $N_\mathrm{Mg}$ at fixed $X_c$.
Production runs use 16 MPI ranks.

\paragraph*{Initial condition (key methodological choice).}
For each $(T, X_c)$ run we use a \emph{Fermi--Dirac-seeded} (\emph{fdseed})
initial condition: $X_\mathrm{GB}(0)$ is set to
$X_\mathrm{GB}^\mathrm{FD}(T, X_c)$ by random Mg placement at GB and
bulk sites in the proportions matching the canonical FD prediction.
The trajectory is initialised \emph{at} the FD hypothesis. Any net
descent of $X_\mathrm{GB}(t)$ below $X_\mathrm{GB}^\mathrm{FD}$ is
direct evidence that the equilibrium $X_\mathrm{GB}^{\infty} <
X_\mathrm{GB}^\mathrm{FD}$, irrespective of whether the trajectory has
reached strict equilibrium within the wallclock budget. This sidesteps
the much larger equilibration cost otherwise required to bring a
saturated-GB initial condition down to its true equilibrium when the
gap is small.

\paragraph*{Production schedule.}
Each run consists of 10~ps of NVT equilibration (no swaps) followed by
300~ps of HMC production --- $3 \times 10^5$ MD timesteps with
3000 interleaved bursts of 100 swap attempts, for $3 \times 10^5$ total
attempted moves. Per-frame configurations are dumped every 1~ps for
post-processing of $X_\mathrm{GB}(t)$; thermodynamic and swap counters
are logged every 0.1~ps. Restart files are written every 5~ps,
allowing resumption past the SLURM 24~h wallclock limit when the
trajectory is still drifting. The HMC step-by-step is given in
Algorithm~\ref{alg:hmc}.


\subsection{$(T, X_c)$ grid and comparison}\label{sec:meth:grid}

The hypothesis-versus-ground-truth comparison is repeated on a $(T, X_c)$
grid covering $T \in \{300, 500, 700\}$~K and $X_c \in [0.04, 0.30]$.
The dilute-side boundary $X_c \approx 0.04$ is set by closed-box ceiling
compression: at $X_c < 0.03$, the global Mg inventory $N_\mathrm{Mg} =
X_c \cdot N_\mathrm{tot}$ is small enough that even
$X_\mathrm{GB}^\mathrm{FD}$ approaches the geometric ceiling $X_c \cdot
N_\mathrm{tot}/N_\mathrm{GB}$, leaving negligible dynamic range in
which to resolve $X_\mathrm{GB}^\mathrm{HMC} < X_\mathrm{GB}^\mathrm{FD}$.
The reported quantity at each grid point is the gap
$X_\mathrm{GB}^\mathrm{HMC} - X_\mathrm{GB}^\mathrm{FD,canon}$, with
uncertainties described in Sec.~\ref{sec:meth:stats}.


\subsection{Statistical analysis}\label{sec:meth:stats}

Two distinct sources of randomness are reported with two distinct
confidence-interval prescriptions, neither of which is assumed Gaussian.

\paragraph*{Trajectory-level CI on $\langle X_\mathrm{GB}\rangle$.}
The first 20\% of production frames are dropped as burn-in. The
post-burn-in mean and 95\% confidence interval are computed by
stationary block bootstrap with a 5-frame block size and $2000$
resamples, accounting for the autocorrelation between adjacent
1~ps dump frames.

\paragraph*{Per-site empirical occupation $\hat P_i$ in
Fig.~\ref{fig:repulsion}.}
Each GB site in a converged HMC snapshot is a Bernoulli trial
(Mg or Al, 0/1). For Wagih-favourable sites with $\Delta E_i \in
[-30, -15]$~kJ/mol, we sub-bin by the local Mg neighbour count
$n^\mathrm{Mg}_\mathrm{local}$ (Mg atoms within $r \leq 5$~\AA) and
report the empirical occupation $\hat P$ per integer
$n^\mathrm{Mg}_\mathrm{local}$ with its 95\% Wald binomial confidence
interval [Eq.~(\ref{eq:WaldCI})]. The two CI prescriptions therefore
treat distinct sources of randomness: trajectory autocorrelation in
the former, finite-sample Bernoulli noise in the latter.

\section{Atomistic system and anneal schedule}\label{app:atomistic}

The Al polycrystal is generated by drawing 16 grain seeds uniformly
within the $200^3$~\AA$^3$ box and a uniformly random orientation
$R \in \mathrm{SO}(3)$ for each grain, then assigning every lattice
site to its nearest seed under periodic boundary conditions (3D
Voronoi tessellation, implemented via $k$-d-tree nearest-neighbour
lookup). The FCC lattice constant is $a = 4.05$~\AA. The anneal
schedule follows Wagih \emph{et al.}~\cite{Wagih2020}:
(1) conjugate-gradient minimisation; (2) NVT temperature ramp from
100~K to $0.4\,T_\mathrm{melt} = 373$~K over 50~ps; (3) NPT hold at
373~K for 250~ps to relax the GB structure; (4) $-3$~K/ps quench to
0~K; (5) final box relaxation by conjugate gradient with full Hessian
update on the simulation cell. The resulting annealed configuration is
the fixed substrate for all subsequent measurements. GB atoms are
identified by adaptive common-neighbour analysis~\cite{Stukowski2012}
with default cutoffs.


\section{Key equations}\label{app:eq}

\paragraph*{Per-site segregation energy} (Sec.~\ref{sec:meth:base}):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:dE}
\Delta E_i = E_i^\mathrm{GB-Mg} + E^\mathrm{bulk-Al}
           - E_i^\mathrm{GB-Al} - E^\mathrm{bulk-Mg},
\end{equation}
the energy change for transferring a Mg atom from a bulk
substitutional site to GB site $i$, with all four energies obtained
at $T = 0$~K with full local relaxation.

\paragraph*{Wagih single-site Fermi--Dirac}
(Sec.~\ref{sec:meth:FD}):
\begin{equation}\label{eq:FD_single}
P_i^\mathrm{FD}(\Delta E_i, T, \Delta\mu) =
\frac{1}{1 + \exp\!\big[(\Delta E_i + \Delta\mu)/k_B T\big]}.
\end{equation}
$P_i^\mathrm{FD}$ is the probability that GB site $i$ is occupied by
Mg, treated as independent of all other GB sites --- the dilute-limit
(independent-site) assumption tested by HMC.

\paragraph*{Closed-box (canonical) FD}
(Sec.~\ref{sec:meth:FD}):
At fixed $X_c$, $\Delta\mu$ is determined self-consistently from
$N_\mathrm{Mg}$ conservation:
\begin{equation}\label{eq:FD_canon}
\sum_{i \in \mathrm{GB}} P_i^\mathrm{FD}(\Delta E_i, T, \Delta\mu)
+ N_\mathrm{bulk} \cdot
\frac{1}{1 + \exp(\Delta\mu/k_B T)}
= X_c \cdot N_\mathrm{tot}.
\end{equation}
The first sum is over all $N_\mathrm{GB}$ sites with their respective
$\Delta E_i$; the second term is the bulk Mg population (treating bulk
sites as $\Delta E = 0$). $X_\mathrm{GB}^\mathrm{FD,canon} =
(1/N_\mathrm{GB}) \sum_{i \in \mathrm{GB}} P_i^\mathrm{FD}$ once
$\Delta\mu$ is determined.

\paragraph*{Wald 95\% binomial confidence interval}
(Sec.~\ref{sec:meth:stats}):
For $n$ Bernoulli trials with empirical success rate $\hat P$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:WaldCI}
\mathrm{CI}_{95\%}(\hat P) =
\hat P \pm 1.96 \sqrt{\hat P (1-\hat P) / n}.
\end{equation}
The Wald approximation breaks down at the boundaries
($\hat P \approx 0$ or $1$); for those bins we report $[0, 0]$ or
$[1, 1]$ explicitly in the figure.


\section{HMC sampling protocol}\label{app:alg}

\begin{algorithm}[H]
\caption{HMC sampling at $(T, X_c)$ with fdseed initial condition.}
\label{alg:hmc}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\State Solve Eq.~(\ref{eq:FD_canon}) for $\Delta\mu(T, X_c)$ on the
       $\{\Delta E_i\}$ spectrum
\State Compute $X_\mathrm{GB}^\mathrm{FD}(T, X_c) = (1/N_\mathrm{GB})
       \sum_i P_i^\mathrm{FD}$
\State \textbf{fdseed IC:} place $\lfloor X_\mathrm{GB}^\mathrm{FD}
       \cdot N_\mathrm{GB} \rfloor$ Mg atoms at random GB sites; place
       the remaining $N_\mathrm{Mg} = X_c \cdot N_\mathrm{tot}$ Mg
       atoms at random bulk sites
\State Equilibrate 10\,ps of NVT MD at temperature $T$ (no swaps)
\For{$k = 1$ to $3000$}
    \Comment{$3 \times 10^5$ MD steps total $\Rightarrow$ 300\,ps}
    \State Run 100 NVT MD timesteps (0.1\,ps, $\Delta t = 1$\,fs)
    \State Attempt 100 Mg$\leftrightarrow$Al type-swap moves; accept each
           with $P=\min\{1, \exp(-\Delta U/k_B T)\}$ at temperature $T$
    \If{$k \bmod 10 = 0$} \Comment{every 1\,ps}
        \State Dump per-atom \texttt{(id, type)} for $X_\mathrm{GB}(t)$
    \EndIf
    \If{$k \bmod 50 = 0$} \Comment{every 5\,ps}
        \State Write restart checkpoint
    \EndIf
\EndFor
\State \textbf{Post-process:} discard first 20\% of production frames as
       burn-in; compute $\langle X_\mathrm{GB}\rangle$ and 95\% CI by
       stationary block bootstrap (5-frame block, 2000 resamples)
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}


\section{$\Delta E$ spectrum convergence and Wagih comparison}\label{app:dE_compare}

The choice $n = 500$ GB sites is supported by two checks:
(1) the skew-normal fit parameters $\{\mu, \sigma, \alpha\}$ are
converged below the precision relevant to the canonical FD predictor,
which depends on the spectrum integral rather than the fit form per
se; (2) a two-sample Kolmogorov--Smirnov test on $\{\Delta E_i\}$
versus the Wagih Zenodo dataset (their $n = 82{,}646$ DFT-trained
machine-learning predictions on a comparable polycrystal) yields
$p > 0.5$, indicating spectrum-level indistinguishability under our
chosen tightness of CG relaxation. The KS-stability and skew-normal
parameter drift across $n \in \{50, 100, 250, 500\}$ are detailed
elsewhere; the spectrum match is also illustrated in
Fig.~\ref{fig:spectrum}.
